# FUTURE_DS_02
Customer Retention and Churn Analysis 
Overview:

This project focuses on analyzing customer subscription data to understand patterns in customer retention, churn behavior, and revenue performance. The aim is to uncover key factors that contribute to customer loss and provide data-driven insights that can support strategic decision-making and improve retention.

Objectives:

The main objectives of this project are to measure churn rate, identify high-risk customers, and analyze how different factors such as subscription plans, billing frequency, and customer lifecycle events influence churn. The project also evaluates how churn impacts overall revenue.

Data Preparation:

The dataset was preprocessed using Python to ensure accuracy and consistency. This included handling missing values, correcting data types, removing duplicates, and standardizing categorical variables. Additional validation steps were performed to confirm data quality before analysis.

Analysis:

Exploratory data analysis was conducted to examine customer behavior and relationships between variables. The analysis focused on churn distribution, subscription patterns, billing behavior, and customer activity indicators such as upgrades and downgrades. Relationships between churn and revenue metrics were also explored to understand financial impact.

Key Insights:

The analysis revealed that customers on shorter billing cycles are more likely to churn compared to those on longer-term plans. Trial users showed a higher probability of leaving, suggesting potential gaps in onboarding or product experience. Customers who downgrade their subscriptions tend to have a higher likelihood of churning, indicating dissatisfaction prior to exit. Additionally, churn was found to have a noticeable impact on recurring revenue across certain customer segments.

Tools used:
Python (Pandas, NumPy) for data cleaning and preprocessing
Power BI for data exploration and insight generation
Excel/CSV as the data source

Conclusion:

This project demonstrates how raw customer subscription data can be transformed into meaningful insights that highlight the drivers of churn and retention. By identifying patterns related to billing behavior, subscription changes, and customer engagement, the analysis provides a foundation for improving customer experience and reducing churn. These insights can support businesses in making informed decisions to enhance retention strategies, optimize revenue, and build long-term customer relationships.
